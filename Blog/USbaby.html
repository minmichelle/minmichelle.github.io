Different charts show facts about U.S baby name

There are three different, unique ways to explain the baby name data, let’s take a look.

  • 1. How many boys and girls are born each year?
  • 2. Name diversity since 1880
  • 3. Most popular name over time

Boys and girls are born each year

It was really interesting to find out the trend of the baby name of boys and girls are pretty similar. It looks like more boys given born than the girls since 1980. The time around 1914 and 1939 are the two big booming period for the new born baby. This indicates the World War I and World War II are highly possible will be main factors to influence the baby born rate. In addition, after the World War II, more babies coming and until 1960, the second-high peak of baby born period appears. In the other hand, the great recession during 1930 drop down the born rate but get recovered in the late 1930s.

(The X axis are the time span label from 1980 to 2013. Sorry it was looks a mess since the chart type I choose can only do something like this. However, I am working on the change of another chart API, just need time to make some improvement.)



Name diversity since 1880

We all know the baby are increasing by the time goes as the last chart shows, but will this make influence of the diversity of the name? Will more and more names be being used? Now, you can find the answer by looking at this chart. It clearly demonstrates the diversity name trend is rising each year. It was noticeable that in the past 10 years, the rate has decline for not really a certain reason, but we would like to wait to see the future trend. As we could see in the history period from 1910s to 1930s, the trend inclined as well, but it increased rapidly after the great recession gone.



Most popular girls name over time

From this pie chart we have a general idea that the winner name last for several decades and will be replaced by another name in the next period of time. So basically it winning trend are in a very consistent pattern that every popular name will only be last about a certain period and comes next to another new popular name. Just as the girls name chart, the name Mary last 67 years in a row and finally replaced by Linda (this chart was spanned clockwise direction from 1980 to 2013.) Another fact we know is the name Mary is the name last longest of the most popular name since 1980, which is 67 years.
